Mechanical Design · Sep – Dec 2025

Auto-Measuring Spice Dispenser

About

Designed and prototyped a compact, refillable spice dispenser that uses mechanical components to dispense preset, precise increments — solving a real problem for people who cook with specific recipes or dietary constraints. The project focused on clean mechanical design and reliable actuation in a small form factor.

Highlights

  • Designed a compact refillable mechanism for precise, repeatable spice dispensing
  • Used mechanical components to achieve preset increment control without electronics
  • Prototyped and iterated on form factor for practical everyday use

Biomedical Engineering Capstone · Sponsored by Medtronic · Sep 2022 – May 2023

Spinal Torque Device

About

For my Biomedical Engineering capstone, sponsored by Medtronic, I designed and manufactured a biocompatible adapter for measuring the torque applied to spinal surgical instruments during procedures. Accurate torque measurement is critical for patient safety, and this device filled a gap in intraoperative feedback tools.

Highlights

  • Designed a biocompatible adapter compatible with existing spinal surgical instruments
  • Manufactured the device to meet sterility and materials requirements for surgical use
  • Collaborated with Medtronic stakeholders to align design with clinical needs

Simulation · Jan – May 2023

Drug Eluting Stent Simulation

About

Using COMSOL Multiphysics, I simulated a drug eluting stent to analyze the mechanical stresses on the stent structure and model the kinetics of drug release into surrounding tissue. This type of simulation is essential for optimizing stent geometry and drug coating for cardiovascular applications.

Highlights

  • Modeled structural stresses on the stent under physiological loading conditions
  • Simulated drug release kinetics to analyze delivery rate and tissue penetration
  • Used COMSOL's multiphysics coupling to combine structural and transport phenomena
