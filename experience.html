Summer 2025 · Neocis · Miami, FL

Surgical Instruments Intern

Surgical Robotics · Medical Device

Overview

At Neocis, maker of the Yomi robotic dental surgery system, I worked on adapting oral fiducials — positioning markers used by the robot during surgery — to be compatible with patients who require an oxygen mask. This required balancing clinical constraints, DFMA principles, and aesthetics in a highly regulated environment.

Key Contributions

  • Adapted oral fiducial design to accommodate an oxygen mask while maintaining surgical precision
  • Created and tested fixtures using SolidWorks and 3D printing to precisely place beads in pockets
  • Considered DFMA principles and aesthetic constraints throughout the design process
SolidWorks 3D Printing DFMA Surgical Robotics

Program Design Community Engagement Leadership

Summer 2024 · Smith + Nephew · Pittsburgh, PA

Robotics Intern

Robotics Division · Orthopedic Medical Devices

Overview

Working within Smith + Nephew's robotics team, I focused on the design and testing of bone pins used in shoulder procedures. Using SolidWorks simulations with bone models, I analyzed compressive stresses to inform pin placement and geometry, then validated designs through physical testing.

Key Contributions

  • Designed pins using bone simulations in SolidWorks to identify geometries with minimal compressive stress
  • Conducted compression and deflection testing to optimize bone pin placement for shoulder procedures
  • Contributed to improving surgical outcomes through data-driven design iteration
SolidWorks Biomechanics Orthopedic Devices Testing

Summer 2023 · Smith + Nephew · Memphis, TN

Manufacturing Engineering Intern

Manufacturing & Clean Room Operations

Overview

In the manufacturing arm of Smith + Nephew, I worked in the clean room environment programming implants and introducing new software to production workflows. This role gave me deep exposure to regulated manufacturing processes and documentation standards for FDA-approved medical devices.

Key Contributions

  • Programmed 135 knee and hip zirconium oxide implants using VEI software to validate part size and family
  • Created VRA documentation and work instructions to introduce new VEI vision software to the clean room
  • Maintained compliance with regulated manufacturing standards throughout all work
VEI Software Clean Room Implant Manufacturing Technical Writing
